Situbondo

The Regency is located on the north coast of Java , in the Tapal Kuda area and is surrounded by sugar cane plantations, tobacco, Baluran protected forests and fishing business locations. With its strategic location, in the middle of the Java-Bali land transportation route, its economic activities appear to be active. Situbondo has the famous Panarukan port as the eastern end of the Anyer-Panarukan Pos Highway on the island of Java , built by Daendels in the Dutch colonial era.

History Of Situbondo Regency

That said, Situbondo in ancient times was a lake or there. In the heyday of the Javanese kingdoms, Situbondo was part of conflicts over the territorial struggle and power of the Majapahit kingdom with the Blambangan kingdom , and it was in this area that the Paregreg war was believed to be part of the Majapahit destruction. Situbondo residents come from various tribes, the majority are from the Madura tribe . In the 1950s to 1970s, economic life was largely supported by the sugar industry with 6 plantations and sugar mills around it, namely in Asembagus , Panji , Olean, Wringin Anom, Demas, and Prajekan. With the decline of the sugar industry in the 1980s and 1990s , economic activity shifted towards fishing. Breeding business and shrimp enlargement become the foundation of the community. Manalagi mango, yam , and arumanis of Situbondo very well-known and much sought after by fans of the fruit . Until now the economic potential of the mango plantation is still handled by the home industry, not on a plantation industry scale. Some other potential natural resources are still "unemployed". It is suspected that the oil content in Situbondo Regency (around Olean) is quite abundant. The people of Situbondo are waiting for investors to come and explore the natural wealth which until now is "still hidden". East Java people are familiar with Situbondo from the Pasir Putih beach, a beach recreation area located approximately 23 km west of Situbondo. White Sand is famous for its sloping beaches and white sand. in the 1960s to 1970s there were still many marine habitats that could be found on this beach. Seahorses and beautiful colorful corals are sold at local aquariums, but now these creatures cannot be found anymore.

The change of name

Initially the name Situbondo was Panarukan district with the capital city of Situbondo, so that during the Dutch administration by Governor General Daendels (± th 1808-1811) who built roads by forced labor along the north coast of Java island known as "Jalan Anyer - Panarukan" or better known as " Daendels Street ", then over time it was only during the reign of Regent Achmad Tahir (± th 1972) that it was changed to Situbondo Regency with the capital city of Situbondo based on Republic of Indonesia Government Regulation Number. 28/1972 concerning Change of Name and Change of Regional Government's Domicile. Situbondo Regent's residence in the past has not been in the Regency Hall, but still occupies his private home. During the reign of Regent Raden Aryo Poestoko Pranowo (c. 1900-1924), he improved the Regency Hall while building the Regent's Residence and the Regent's Aide Pavilion until now, then during the Regent's Administration Drs. H. Moh. Diaaman, Situbondo Regency Government revised Regency Hall (± 2002).

Pembagian administratif

Cities

The capital of Situbondo Regency is Situbondo Subdistrict, but in order to improve the welfare of the community it also improves infrastructure such as, construction and improvement of markets, city parks, traffic, and others. Situbondo Regency Government has also inaugurated the Second City of Situbondo, which is the Besuki District in the western region and the Asembagus District in the east.
